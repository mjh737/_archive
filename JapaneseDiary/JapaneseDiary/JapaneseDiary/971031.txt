Friday 31st October 
Got up at 7 to go to the maintains on my own.  Mrs Saiki worked out all my train connections for me.  I had no money so I went to the bank but the cashpoints don’t open until 8.45 so I had to go home and get her to sort out the trains again.  The journey took about 90 mins and a guy next to me chatted to me in English for ages but I just wanted to read.  The mountain path was clearly marked on my map but disappeared in reality after only 200m.  I ended up climbing a 60˚ slope for an hour in dense forest.  At the top of this small mountain (400m) I could see my destination (900m) but had spent so much time on the early stages I decided not to risk it so I walked along the ridge for a bit.  I found a slope which led down to the stream but it was like wet sand so I kept slipping down 50 meters at a time – very scary.  I kept grabbing branches which would snap and send me another 50 meters down – it was just like a film.  Halfway down there was a big waterfall.  To avoid climbing through the forest again, I had to climb down the waterfall which was really dangerous.  About 10 feet from the bottom I saw one of the killer bees that I’ve heard so much about.  It was 3-4 inches long and, whereas English bees are yellow, this was bright red and sounded like a learjet.  It flew past my head and went in a hole.  A few seconds later it flew off but I wasn’t going to hang around so I jumped from sheer panic the 10 feet to the bottom.  The journey became hard again after that – dense woodland.  I could hear growling in the wood at one point and hurried on not wishing to encounter the bears which my companion on the train this morning told me frequent these parts.  I finally ended up in civilisation at about 4 o’clock.  I’m so tired now I don’t think I will get up until Monday.
