Wednesday 25th March
I had to get up at the crack of dawn today (7am) and get ready for the Sadō trip – I’ve tried to go twice before but both times something bad happened and it was cancelled.  I had to meet R&B at Bandai Guchi to catch the 8.45 bus.  Not having a watch I blindly followed them onto a bus but ten minutes later when I realised we were going east instead of west I asked the time and figured that we were on the wrong bus so we ran back to the station and got a taxi.  The ferry was huge and laid out inside like the Titanic with a big staircase.  We stood outside for most of the 2 ½ hour journey and fed the seagulls which followed the boat.  When we got to the island we realised it was merely a godforsaken sea port off the north coast of Japan whereas we were expecting some tropical paradise or utopia.  We walked around the deserted streets for an hour before finding a restaurant right next to the port.  After eating we wandered around again but soon realised that there was nothing to do but drink so B and I bought some cans and sat in a park like a couple of tramps.  We waited for the ferry which left at 6.40pm having done nothing all day.  On the way back we sat at the back of the boat drinking and chatting.  It finally pulled into Niigata at about 9.00 and we alighted and decided to walk back to the station which didn’t take too long.  We tried to get a coffee but everywhere was closed so we had a couple of games of Victory Lap before going home.  By this time depression had set in so I walked home a really long way, feeling sorry for myself and eventually got to bed at 11.30pm.
