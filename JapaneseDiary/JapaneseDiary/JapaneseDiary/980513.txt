13th May
I started getting ready at about 10am and finally left the house at 5.45pm with a warm whisky glow in my stomach.  When I got to Niigata I took out some money and bought a disposable camera so I can prove to people that she really is the most beautiful girl in the world.  In fact, when she turned up I was absolutely gob-smacked and barely recognised her.  We went to the Italian restaurant and, on the way, chatted and got to know each other a little better.  The meal was okay but I couldn’t eat – I just sat and stared into her eyes.  We discovered that we both like karaoke so afterwards we went for an hour and a half of singing together.  Unfortunately she likes Aerosmith and Bon Jovi so I had to sing ‘Angel’, ‘Always’ and ‘Bed of Roses’ to impress her.  I was on top form and she too had a heavenly voice and we smiled at each other lots.  Many photos were taken and I am anxious to get the film developed.  After that I caught the bus home with her barely resisting the temptation to declare my undying love for her.  I walked her to her front door and with a peck on the cheek I departed and commenced the ¾ hour walk back to Kobari. I got to bed at 12.45am.  I have arranged to see her next Thursday which is her next free day.  During the daytime she has college everyday and her evenings are spent working apart from two evenings a week.  I’ve left the ball in her court in respect of what we are going to do so I’ll look forward to a surprise.  She reckons she wants to come to England next year and I have made the great sacrifice of saying I will be delighted to meet her and show her the proverbial ropes.  If I see her once a week until I got home then we have six more evenings together.
