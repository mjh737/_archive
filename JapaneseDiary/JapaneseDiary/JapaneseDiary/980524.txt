Sunday 24th May
After a sleepless night, I got out of bed at 4.30am and showered/had breakfast before catching the first train into Niigata.  I phoned Dad and said don’t phone tomorrow because it will still be today here and I’m in Tokyo.  I bought some supplies and caught the 7.00 bus to the capital.  It was a boring journey only mildly improved by the presence of a fine example of Japanese femininity in the seat next to me.  I waited at Ikebukuro for a short time before Naoko (the quiet one) met me.  I had to make difficult conversation at her for 30 minutes while we travelled to Ebisu to meet the other two.  It was good to see them but conversation did not last long.  We had dinner in an Italian restaurant on some famous street before going to Shibuya to meet another girl, Yūko who was at Jon’s Christmas party last year or whenever.  We had another coffee somewhere and went to a game centre for a bit before it started pouring down with rain when we decided to go back to Ebisu and to an English pub.  It was a cool place, just like an English pub and no Americans in sight.  The only draw back was that the Guinness as well as all the normal Jap lagers was £5 a pint.  I bought one and had one bought for me – it was heavenly.  I got talking to a couple of wide boy dealer blokes and one of them went to school with Simon Levett who used to work at Lloyds.  Both of them were from the Medway ‘tans’ and it was good to chat.  Afterwards me, Misa and the pregnant Yūri went for 90 mins of karaoke before buying some take-away rāmen and returning to Misa’s flat to eat it and drink English tea.  That was almost as good as the Guinness.  We went to sleep at about three or four.
