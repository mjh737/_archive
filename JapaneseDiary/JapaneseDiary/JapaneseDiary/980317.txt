Tuesday 17th March
Had a bit of a hangover this morning so I decided to stay in all day which I actually thoroughly enjoyed.  I was happy to be in my own little world writing out my kanji cards and reading my new book which I bought yesterday.  It’s a book on how to speak ōsaka dialect.  It was fascinating and I learnt a lot about standard Japanese too.  I had a nice dinner and tried to engage the Saikis in a conversation about dialects and, in particular, Niigata dialect but response was not forthcoming and we had to talk about salmon for the zillionth time.  After dinner, I walked Pao or, should I say, ran Pao – I must have run 2 miles and, although my legs ache a little now, I didn’t find it too taxing at the time.  When I got back the Psychos disappeared into their little room so I came back upstairs to my little world.  Somedays I get so bored because I have no friends here apart from Rich and Balàzs but some days I love my own company and even though I spend a lot of time thinking about Kate and Rob and Mat and Geoff and KT etc I do so with a pleasant glow in my chest because I only have 100 days to go before I can be with them again.  I have decided to write a diary for the rest of my life.  At home, I have a diary from 1985 which always provides interesting reading despite the fact that there are only a couple of lines for each entry which say something like ‘played lego, went to school, had a shit’ followed by an extensive list in TV guide form of the days viewing.  I wonder if this diary will be interesting in 10 years time or whether it will just seem sad (in both senses of the word).  Now I think I shall do a few more kanji – I seem to enjoy writing them out far more than actually learning them but I guess they must be sinking in.
