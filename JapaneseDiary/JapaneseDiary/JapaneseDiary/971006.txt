Monday 6th October
Met Rich and Balazs at Niigata Daigaku Mae Eki and went to university to sort out modules and banking.  Somehow we ended up with a Criminal Law course but I guess it’ll be quite interesting..  We also got our student cards, our library cards and a co-op card (which cost 500 yen but the purpose of which is still a mystery).  We waited around for an hour before meeting Saiko, who was at Cardiff last year, and her friends who spoke English too.  We had lunch and spoke English which is not really why I am here but at least it was a break.  Then Tomu-san took us to the amusements, as usual, and the Post Office.  I will shortly be sending everyone a postcard as a direct consequence of that very exciting and useful adventure.  For dinner I had some kind of fish which, surprisingly, was cooked, along with rice and the usual bowl of miso soup.  They made me try some nattoo (pickled beans) and some salted apricot which the Japanese love but, as I was later informed, is not to the delight of the Western palette.  Oh how we laughed!!
