24th June 
It’s strange to think that in a few hours more than a week I will be in England.  It’s like sometimes when you dream you become aware of the time and think ‘Oh, I’ll be waking up soon’.  It’s like that now: in my Japanese dream I have become aware of the sun coming up and know that it will be over in a little while.  Today I got up late, studied until I’d finished my dissertation research and then went to the Usui’s for the final lesson.   Apart from the decorations it was the same as ever for the first 3 hours.  However, Ayumu has become impossible to control and the 2 hours I spend with him and Yoshimi, we just play.   However, at 7am Yoshimis mum and the Takizawa’s turned up and we had a feast of a party.  I was asked about my dissertation and my opinion of Japanese law.  I answered diplomatically but when I asked the opinion of Japanese people never discuss it – weird.  Anyway at 9.30pm I left and it was quite a sad occasion and now, having said my first goodbyes, I really feel like I’m going home – it’s a bit depressing.
