Tuesday 28th April
Lethargy did not set in until 2pm today so I took the opportunity to write some letters for the first 4 hours of the day.  I still have four more to write though.  When lethargy did set in though, it came fast and hard and invaded all parts of my body rendering me useless for everything apart from reading ‘For Whom the Bell Tolls’ which I have probably done for about 5 hours in all.  No matter how much of it I read I never seem to be any closer to the end.  Mrs Saiki came home at 6.00pm and told me she had bought me a Jap-style bentō take-away for dinner and that she was off out.  I said ‘thank you’ and these are the only words to have escaped my lips all day.  I walked Pao and now am about to read again.  There is nothing else to report so I shall bid you a goodnight.
