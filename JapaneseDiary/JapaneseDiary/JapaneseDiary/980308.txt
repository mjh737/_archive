Sunday 8th March
We got up at 7.00 and had a boiled egg each for breakfast.  I went up the shop to get a load of sweets in for our car journey but I ended up scoffing the lot (about 400 boiled sweets) on my own.  We set off at 8 o’clock and I mentioned to Mr Saiki that Dad was into bird-watching so our first port of call was the ‘Swan Lake’ that I went to last year with him and Balàzs.  We next stopped at a temple/shrine situation which, to me, was just like the 36 other temple/shrine situations which I’ve seen.  However, Mum and Dad seemed to enjoy it.  Right next to the temple was an ancient snow plough steam train which Mr Saiki, quite rightly, thought Dad would be fascinated by.  We then moved on to this town which is renowned for rāmen where Mum and Dad had to go through public chopstick mal-coordination but I think they enjoyed the few mouthfuls they achieved.  Next up was a sake factory where we had a Japanese guide all of whose Japanese I understood.  I did a contemporaneous interpretation which was a very rewarding experience.  We had a few testers at the end and Mum loved it although she was visibly and detrimentally affected by it.  We went to some other kind of Japanese ‘place’ and then to a sort of ‘selling things’ Japanese ‘place’ (maybe a shop) before visiting the castle where I went in my first week.  After that it started to get dark as unfortunately we couldn’t go to the samurai house so we started home.  We were silent for most of the journey but then had dinner at a sushi bar complete with conveyor belt service.  We came back very tired at about 9 o’clock and Mum and Dad went to bed pretty soon.  I stayed up and studied for a bit but now I think I shall join them (kind of).
