Thursday 10th March
Disaster struck.  I was rudely awakened at 1.30 in the morning by Dad saying Mum was ill and we would have to cancel Sadō.  This is the second time since I’ve been here that I’ve been looking forward to a trip to Sadō Island only for it to be cancelled at the last minute.  I had to get up and write a note for Mr Saiki who was to get up at 4.00am and give us a lift to the port.  After that I spent ages trying to get back to sleep and when I finally did, Mrs Saiki woke me up to find out if Mum was all right.  After that, I spent ages trying to get back to sleep and when I finally did, Mr Saiki woke me up to find out if Mum was all right.  After that, I spent ages trying to get back to sleep and when I finally did, my alarm clock went off and I had to get up.  Anyway, we had quite a good day.  We went and looked around Hakusan Shrine which was actually quite interesting for a shrine.  We went into Furumachi and did a bit of shopping and had lunch in the Japanese restaurant in the underground shopping complex.  We had our photo taken in the Japanese printclub machine and, at 5 o’clock, went to the cinema to see the new Spielberg film, The Amistad, which was very good but very Spielberg.  Actually, it wasn’t very good.  It was just a bit good but it passed the time in an enjoyable manner.  After that, we made the trek into Bandai and went to the rock’n’roll diner for expensive food and beer and to my surprise, my parents actually liked the food, the waitress in their short nurses uniforms (especially my Dad) and the atmosphere.  After that, I realised that I use the conjunction ‘after that’ far too many times in my diary entries and, after that, we came home and went to bed.
