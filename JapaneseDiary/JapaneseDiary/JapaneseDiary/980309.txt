Monday 9th March
We got up at about 8 o’clock, I think, and took the train to uni so I could show them (Mum and Dad) a bleak grey brick building (the law school), a bleak grey brick building (the library) and a bleak grey brick building (the refectory).  After that we went back to the station and caught a train into town.  Dad and I had a quick game on Victory Lap – he won (yeah right!!).  We went to change up some toraborzu chekku (travellers cheques) but neither of them had bought passports so it was a little tricky but we managed Mum’s.  Dad’ll have to wait.  We went to the ‘good chef’ for a snack for lunch before searching all the department stores for shoes for my birthday but to no avail.  Dad and I went up the Rainbow Tower (Mum stayed on terra firma) and then we all headed off to Furumachi where we did the department store thing again and generally looked around.  Of course, we did the Next 21 skyscraper and even Mum came up.  There, I saw Tera, my personal tutor and Hiroshi, Balàzs’ host brother’s friend.  This was on top of the Chinese girl from my Japanese lecture (not literally ‘on top of’.  I mean ‘in addition to’.  We saw her on the train into town.)  After  that we had a coffee before doing a tour of the underground shopping centre and then moving on to the Royal Host Restaurant for dinner.  Isn’t it typical?  The evening a saucy ‘Right up my cup of tea’ Japanese girl gives me the eye and starts waving at me and giggling with her friend, I’m sitting there with my parents with my Dad eating a big ice cream.  Never mind.  We caught the train home, chatted to the Saikis a little and got an early night for the Sadō trip tomorrow.
