Wednesday 4th March
Dossed around until 1.30 and then cycled to my lesson which was very tiring today.  When I returned home at 8.30 I was absolutely knackered but the worst was yet to come.  Mrs Saiki (later to be reinforced with Mr Saiki) tried to change my travel arrangements suggesting hundreds of options each with their own advantages like it’s a pound cheaper or the bus/train stops at one stop less.  The conclusion was that my original plan is the best and I should not try to change it!! At that I managed to escape.  This is one thing that we foreigners have noticed about the Japanese.  They spend hours planning the simplest excursion, e.g. a trip to a bar and come up with a plan that we would have suggested at the onset as being the easiest and most obvious.  I was grateful for the Saiki’s time but I could have done without the frustration.
