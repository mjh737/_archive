Thursday 11th June
Today was horrendously dull.  Having only 20 days left you would think that time was rushing by but, on the contrary, time is moving deadly slow, almost as if it is teasing me.  I seem to be just waiting around and have no plans until next Wednesday when I shall see the Usu’s for the penultimate time.  I did some dissertation and read some of my new Richard North Paterson book, Degree of Guilt.  I had dinner with the Saiki’s in silence, Scotland having lost their first game in the world cup last night only adding to my misery.  I walked Pao a different route tonight to kill time and didn’t get back until nine.  Had a coffee downstairs, again in silence.  I made my excuses and came up.  I will probably go to bed soon, read a bit and then sleep early to kill some more time.  Today I buried my Yūmi photos to be discovered sometime in the future when the memories have faded.
