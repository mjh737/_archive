Wednesday 3rd June
Felt quite rough most of today.  In the morning I studied a bit and in the afternoon went to the Usui’s for a five hour teaching stint.  Despite the winds being gale force 12 on the beaufort scale and, consequently, it being like trying to cycle through a cliff, I was not late.  I struggled through the first 3 hours barely resisting shouting at the kids or resorting to methods of torture to restrain them, I had a nice dinner though but by the time I got to the Takizawa’s I was dead on my feet.  Fortunately, for all concerned, this was the last Takizawa lesson.  I’m sure their very nice but they “don’t” understand (not: “don’t” not 2can’t” – ‘therefore principle’ – ask me for a definition or see end of diary for abridged version).  I came home, walked Pao then chatted to the Saikis.  Mr Saiki told me I was great at Japanese although we blatantly never understand a word of the other says.
