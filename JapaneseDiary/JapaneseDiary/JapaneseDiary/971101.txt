The very first day of November in the year of our Lord 1997
It’s Saturday night and, like Friday, I’ve stayed in – there’s nothing to do.  I’m in my room – it’s 8.41pm – I’m listening to The Verve and I’ve got my heater on for the first time since arriving.  It’s cold now and it’s rained quite a bit.  We’re expecting snow this month – apparently in the country they get up to 5 metres but only about 50cm here – still that’s more than England.  Today I went into Niigata with R&B and bought some stationery, a Jappo grammar book and complicated vocab book.  We went to McDonalds for lunch despite being on strict instructions from Dr Rowley only to eat Jap food.  The temptation was too great for all concerned.  It was about the same price as England but nowhere near as nice.  The chicken lacked that certain something that only westerners with their poor cuisine can create and, indeed, appreciate.
