Sunday 10th May
Today I have been afflicted with a terrible sadness, a weltshmerz beyond what I usual experience after my countless anticlimactic evenings out.  I can only imagine that it has something to do with losing my key and the fact that I have been singing ‘She came in through the bedroom window’ off Abbey Road all day.  Before I got back last night I was thinking to myself how, for a change, nothing had gone wrong so far and, therefore, imagined that I would not feel so down the following day.  However, even my Dad, when he phoned at seven, instantly picked up on my misery.  I don’t think the Saiki’s know that I broke in but I had to be very careful how I replied to their interrogation about my homecoming last night.  My heart was racing.  I didn’t want to openly admit it, especially to Mr Saiki, but I didn’t want to lie so I just answered their questions truthfully, waiting for my answers to instil suspicion and more probing questions but none were forthcoming so I think I did okay.  However, my main worry now is how I’m going to get another key.  I guess I’ll just have to admit I’ve lost it tomorrow and offer to get a new one cut.  I spent this morning in bed with hayfever and, surprisingly, no hangover.  In the afternoon I read a bit and then slept for a couple of hours.  After dinner Mum and Dad phoned and, even though I’ve done quite a lot over the last two weeks, couldn’t think of anything to say.  This is no doubt due to my sadness.  I walked Pao and played my sickness card when I got back so I could come straight back upstairs.  I will read my book now and maybe watch the Grand Prix in my little TV at 11.45pm.
