Saturday 20th June
Went to bed at 6.00am and got up at twelve for breakfast.  At 1.00pm Mrs Saiki got up and said ‘Good Morning’ and I replied sardonically with the same expression.  After that I came up to spend a day of solitude for a change.  Ir ead mostly and did a telegram crossword.  I had a banana sandwich for lunch – one which I had purchased on my way home this morning and a delicacy which I have only just discovered in the convenience stores here.  After dinner at eight o’clock, I walked Pao and had a fleeting love affair (about 5 seconds) with a beautiful girl in her mid-twenties who smiled warmly when we caught each others eye.  I smiled back and glowed internally for a good 10 minutes afterwards.  Another of those myriad love affairs that never happens.  Now I am once again alone in my room wishing that the last eleven days would not seem as daunting as they do.
