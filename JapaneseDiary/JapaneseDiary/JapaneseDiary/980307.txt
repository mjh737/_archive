Saturday 7th March
I had to wait around for an hour and a half until 6.00am before the train departed.  When it did, I realised that my ticket was wrong, so I had to pay an extra £10 to rectify it.  However, it meant I got to Narita airport at 7.30 rather than 9.00 which was preferable to be on the safe side.  The plane was 20 minutes early but Mum and Dad still didn’t appear until gone 9.00am.  I was a bit emotional just before seeing them but managed to regain control of my masculine side in time for a rapid greeting.  We went to a restaurant for breakfast and there were so many things to say that there was not one conversation but many going on simultaneously.  We went down and booked the train tickets for the next week which was a nice £400 and a nightmare experience due to the complexity of our travel arrangements.  We caught the 10.15 out of Narita which rolled into Tokyo at 11.15.  A short walk and a short wait later, we caught the 11.40 express to Niigata which got in at 1.45.  We went to a little pizza restaurant for snacks and drinks before phoning my host Dad who met us at Kobari station and took us home.  There, we had tea in the guest room before host Mum got back.  She got things organised and made the futons up so Mum and Dad could sleep for a couple of hours while I had a shower.  Despite their flight, I have actually been up 6 hours longer than them.  I will probably feel it tomorrow.  At about 7.00pm we had a nice meal with the Saiki’s and everyone got on very well and laughed a lot.  Mum felt ill and it was difficult to explain it was not because of the food Mrs Saiki had prepared.  Dad and I walked the dog and then went to our rooms.  I am a little depressed whereas I should be happy, the reason being in 6 days time I will be back where I was yesterday, bored and terribly lonely.
