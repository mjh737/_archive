Saturday 23rd May
...to find Mr Saiki sitting in the porch reading the newspaper.  He said he was shocked to see me and I had to quickly concoct an alibi involving staying at Rachels and no marinal antics.  I went to bed for four hours but told Mr S that I was studying.  After that I lay awake for a couple of hours before Mrs Saiki called me down for lunch with the family.  All afternoon I read and slept a bit, whiling away the hours of hangover and looking forward to tomorrow’s Tokyo trip.  Dinner was nice and fortunately I didn’t have to talk much.  Mrs Saiki asked how my date went on Thursday which was quite sociable of her.  I watched a bit of TV before walking Pao and then a little more TV before bidding Mrs Saiki farewell and coming up here to pack.  I have now done this and am currently writing my diary.  After this, at about 9.30, I shall retire.
