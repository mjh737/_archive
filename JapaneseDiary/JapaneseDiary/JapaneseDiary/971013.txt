Monday 13th October
I bought a new red pen tomorrow (if you see what I mean – I wrote this on 14th October 1997).  It’s good isn’t it.  Today is Rachel’s birthday.  We went for a Chinese after uni. It was £2.50 each but Rachel paid anyway.  We played drinking games to the point of tedium and then went back with them to International House for more beer and chatting and stuff.  It was okay really.  I got home (pissed cycling) at around 12.30 a bit worse for wear.
The Chinese food was okay for the price but the problem with the Japs’ food (home-cooked, traditional, Chinese or whatever) is that you only get about four bits of meat if you’re lucky.  It’s ridiculous.  I’m looking forward to getting home and eating a chicken breast or two but probably by that time I’ll be addicted to raw fish, snails and that bloody awful Natto stuff that they love so dearly.  Why can’t you get real food like chips in this Godforsaken land of chopstick-wielding tastebud-deficient short people.
