3rd November – Monday
Met Balàzs at Kobari station at 9.00 and came back here to study for a couple of hours.  We then went to the university festival again.  There was a band on who are, apparently, quite big in Japan (tonight) but all Jappo bands tend to be 80’s rock and this one was no exception, the lead singer thinking he was all of Aerosmith rolled into one hideous seething monster.  Balàzs and I chatted up a couple of girls and drunkenly gave out our phone numbers on the pretence that we would teach English.  Beer went down to 50p a can towards the end of the evening, which is incredibly cheap, so we took advantage of this state of affairs and purchased many. When I got home I was pleased to find ‘Mum’ in a similar state having consumed one can.  We had a good conversation for a couple of hours about the Japanese class system and, in particular, the outcasts known as Eta.  It was very interesting and I was surprised she told me anything since it was blatant that she was ashamed of them.
